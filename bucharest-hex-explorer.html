How to read this

Reading the texture

Every 300 m hexagon over the city is measured six ways, from satellite imagery, census counts and street data.

Single layer

Pick one quality — Greenness, Crowding, Traffic edge, Park reach, Air, Built cover — and the whole city recolors by just that one. Each layer button doubles as its own legend: the ramp and scale under its name show the range, and the arrow shows which direction counts as better.

Livability weave

Turn on the weave to blend five of the metrics into a single score per hex — greener means better all-round. Nothing is combined until you switch it on.

Weight sliders

Once the weave is on, the 0×–3× sliders set how much each quality counts toward it, so the score reflects your priorities rather than a fixed formula. They only affect the weave — turn the weave on first to see them take effect.

Hover and click

Hover any cell to see all six metrics at once. Click to lock the readout in place so you can move the mouse away without losing it.

Limits

These are recent snapshots (2024–2026), not live measurements. Air quality is smoothed to about 1 km, so read it as a district-level signal rather than a street-by-street one. About 27 peripheral cells may read slightly optimistically where OpenStreetMap data is thin.
